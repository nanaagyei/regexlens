# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Junior and mid-level developers who encounter regex they didn't write: in PRs, config files, inherited codebases. They arrive curious, sometimes under time pressure during code review, but mostly wanting to understand a pattern quickly so they can move on with confidence. Senior engineers also use it to verify safety and share review links, but the primary audience is someone still building their regex intuition.

## Product Purpose

RegexLens exists so developers never have to stare at a regex and guess. Paste a pattern, get a plain-English breakdown, safety warnings, and a shareable link. The goal is to make regex review feel as natural as reading a diff: fast, clear, and collaborative. Success looks like a developer who pastes a regex, understands it in under 10 seconds, and shares the analysis link in a PR comment.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, approachable, helpful. Like a sharp colleague who explains things clearly without making you feel slow. No posturing, no jargon walls. RegexLens respects your time: it gives you the answer, then gets out of the way.

Three words: **clear, warm, immediate.**

## Anti-references

- No gradient color treatments (no purple-to-blue, no aurora blobs, no gradient text). Solid colors only.
- No Inter font family. The current Plus Jakarta Sans and Sora are intentional departures from the generic SaaS look.
- No AI-aesthetic slop: no glowing orbs, no particle fields, no "magic sparkle" decorations, no glassmorphism cards.
- No dark-corporate SaaS templates (Stripe clones, generic dashboard marketing pages with hero metrics).
- No walls of feature cards with identical layouts.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the tool, not the pitch.** The landing page should feel like a preview of using RegexLens, not a sales deck. Let the product speak.
2. **Warm confidence.** Not cold and techy, not bubbly and childish. The tone of a friend who happens to be really good at regex.
3. **Immediate clarity.** Every section earns its scroll. No filler, no restated headings, no "we believe" manifestos.
4. **Respect the visit.** People came to solve a problem. Make the path from landing to workbench feel effortless and inviting, not like an obstacle course of marketing sections.
5. **Distinct, not decorative.** Visual identity comes from thoughtful typography, spacing, and color restraint, not from ornamental effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum across all surfaces.
- Color-coded match highlighting must remain distinguishable for common color vision deficiencies (deuteranopia, protanopia). Use shape/position cues alongside color where possible.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: no auto-playing animations without user opt-in.
- Minimum touch targets of 44px on mobile.
- Focus indicators visible on all interactive elements.
